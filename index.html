Program summary

This site explains the public-facing identity, support, consent, privacy, and terms for the SMS program attached to the OpenClaw Family Assistant.

Who receives messages

  • invited family members and approved users only
  • users who separately choose SMS access
  • people who provided their own mobile number
  • users who can opt out at any time

Messages you may receive

  • direct assistant replies you asked for
  • reminders and follow-up notifications

How consent works

SMS consent is collected separately from general access to the service. Invited users are told what messages may be sent before they choose whether to opt in.

Invite path

During invitation or onboarding, SMS uses a separate unchecked consent box with plain-language disclosure and links to the Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.

Keyword path

If keyword enrollment is enabled for an invited user, the follow-up reply confirms the program identity, message types, message frequency, and opt-out and support instructions.

User controls

Message frequency varies by activity. Msg & data rates may apply. Users can reply STOP to opt out or HELP for support at any time.

By opting in, a user agrees to receive recurring conversational support messages, direct assistant replies, reminders, account help, and requested status notifications from OpenClaw Family Assistant operated by Barrett Velker. Consent is optional and not required to use non-SMS access.

Identity, support, and limits

OpenClaw Family Assistant is operated by Barrett Velker as a private, invitation-based service rather than a public marketing program.

Support

reply HELP for support. Invited users may also use the same onboarding or invitation channel that granted access to the service. Support email: support@barrettvelker.com.

Privacy

Personal information is used only to operate the service, deliver requested messages, prevent misuse, and support invited users. It is not sold or shared for third-party marketing.

Service limits

This SMS program is not an emergency service. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages, and availability may vary by device, network, and user settings.
